# Slide: Context — This is the First Wave

## Explaining text (what to say)

This slide sets the **baseline** for Colombia Transfer Out (Bre-B) CSAT. It’s **Wave 1**: the first time we measured satisfaction with the process for sending money in this market. We asked almost 4,000 users: *“¿Cómo calificarías tu nivel de satisfacción con el proceso para enviar dinero?”* and combined their numeric ratings with what they wrote in their own words.

**Rating distribution** shows that 71.8% are satisfied (Alta + Muy alta satisfacción). More than half gave the top score (53.9% — Muy alta satisfacción), but 24.1% gave the lowest (Muy baja satisfacción), which is high and points to a clear group we need to understand and address. The donut and bars make that split visible at a glance.

**Verbatim sentiment** tells us how people *talk* about the experience: 41.5% of comments are positive, 40.3% are improvement-oriented (suggestions, not anger), and only 5.5% are negative. So even among those who write, frustration isn’t the majority — but the 24% who gave the lowest score are a priority for stability and error handling.

Going forward, every new wave will be compared to this Wave 1 baseline so we can see if changes in product and support move the needle.

## TL;DR (um parágrafo, em números)

Na primeira onda da pesquisa (Wave 1), **3.932** pessoas responderam à pergunta de satisfação com o processo de enviar dinero: o CSAT ficou em **71,8%**, puxado por **53,9%** de Muy alta satisfacción e **17,9%** de Alta satisfacción, enquanto **24,1%** deram Muy baja satisfacción (volume alto de detratores). Dos que escreveram comentário, **2.439** verbatims (**62%** da amostra) foram analisados: **41,5%** positivos, **40,3%** de mejora (sugestões) e **5,5%** negativos. Cerca de **38,8%** dos comentários negativos citam instabilidade ou falhas, e ~**107** verbatims falam do 4x1000 — ou seja, a base está clara em números e a prioridade é estabilidade, mensagens de erro e visibilidade do 4x1000 para recuperar os insatisfeitos.
